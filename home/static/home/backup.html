    Projects

    Professional Projects

    Yellow Cards

    This project focused on providing businessmen a platform to spread their information and services easily and quickly. One part of it was to use Computer Vision to automate the textual information extraction of their business cards. We began by testing the existing available Optical Character Recognization software like Tesseract and Easy OCR but even after image processing the presence of the slightest noise or blur in the images resulted in unsatisfactory results. Therefore we started working on building our own OCR using the latest research we found on Text Recognition and Text Detection. Our Custom made OCR used a Convolutional Recurrent Neural Network for character recognizer and CRAFT powered text detector, covering all of English alphabets, numbers, and special characters.

    My major role was developing computer vision models for Detection and Recognition of Text in business cards. I also developed the algorithms that organized the extracted textual information according to their position in the images, futhermore I used LSTM to identify important information like Phone Numbers. I was also involved in building the user authentication system and the datasets for handling user images and extracted textual information.

    ORFOL

    Object Reported Lost and Found (ORFOL) was a project focused on providing people a platform to report their lost items or report found items to the people in their area. The app would recognize the items and help user fill in form quicker. Later the app would match lost and found reports with each other and give suggestions to its users.

    My responsibility was to devise a way to automate the report filling and matching using AI. ORFOL was to recognize nearly 35 commonly lost items automatically to assist the user and extract useful information about the items to help the user fill their report form. I collected, cleaned, annotated, and augmented the entire dataset and used a range of Object detection models to get the best results include DarkNet YOLO, RetinaNet and Faster RCNN. I was also responsible for building the backend for handling input and output of data from the machine learning models which included building and maintaining the databases, building the user authentication and making the algorithm for match Lost and Found reports using Natural Language Processing.

    Vicinality

    Vinivality is a website that can help people find other people with similar social interests based on their bios and location. My responsibility was to develop a friend recommendations system for Vicinality using Natural Language Processing based on interests, age groups, political interest, personality type, and work type. I employed an unsupervised approach to the problem by using K-means clustering algorithm to cluster people based on their provided information and recommendations were given from the top matches from within their vicinity.


    Academic Projects

    Cancer Analysis and Detection using Deep Learning

    (Thesis/ FYP)

    Visit Repo

    This was my Final Year Thesis Project that I completed during my AI Research Fellowship at Pattern Recognition Lab. This project was related to Medical Image Processing and Segmentation. My responsibility was to create an automated pipeline that could detect and segment malignant (cancerous) cells in breast tissue images. I collected a dataset that contained images of 74 patients for training, 24 patients samples for testing, and 11 patients for validation. I used a Mask RCNN for localization and segmentation but the false positive rate was quite high so I added another step for further classification. I created two classes of true-positive and false-positive cells and used transfer learning to trained a CNN. So, whatever cell that was detected from the Mask RCNN was further checked by the CNN which included the f-score from 0.6 to 0.75.
